Who We Are

History of OES

  • Oregon Electric Station had first opened on October 15, 1912, headlining local newspapers declaring it to be "the greatest day in the history of Eugene."

  • The station was originally designed by A. E. Doyle and was completed in 1914 as the final stop of the electric railway, connecting people through out the Willamette Valley.

  • Listed on the National Historic Register, OES has now become a landmark of social significance that has a union of historic value and incredible experiences.

  • Now brings nearly a century of restaurant knowledge gathered from establishments that span the globe, the OES Family is again connecting people by offering memorable dining experiences.

  • Our menus are globally inspired and artistically crafted, striving to use organic, sustainable and locally sourced ingredients.

  • Representing the rich history of Eugene as well as its surrounding fertile lands, Oregon Electric Station is committed and dedicated to quality of product, ambience, and hospitality.
